Suggested Reading

Queenie

by Candice Carty-Williams

£8.99

Open, warm and immensely funny, Carty-Williams’s sparkling debut is an unforgettable coming-of-age tale that explores black female identity and sexual politics in contemporary Britain. Hilarious, characterful and assured, Queenie marks the arrival of an exceptional new talent in fiction.

Hunger: A memoir of my body

by Roxane Gay

£8.99

Roxane Gay writes with intimacy and sensitivity about food and bodies; using her emotional and psychological struggles as a means of expressing anxieties regarding pleasure, consumption, appearance and health. In Hunger, she casts an insightful and critical eye on her childhood, teens, and twenties--including the devastating act of violence that acted as a turning point in her young life--and brings readers into the present and the realities, pains, and joys of her daily life.

Beloved

by Toni Morrison

£8.99

Winner of the Pulitzer Prize, Toni Morrison’s Beloved is a spellbinding and dazzlingly innovative portrait of a woman haunted by the past. Combining the visionary power of legend with the unassailable truth of history, Morrison’s unforgettable novel is one of the great and enduring works of American literature.

All About Love: New Visions

by Bell Hooks

£8.99

renowned scholar, cultural critic and feminist bell hooks offers a proactive new ethic for a society bereft with lovelessness--not the lack of romance, but the lack of care, compassion, and unity. People are divided, she declares, by society’s failure to provide a model for learning to love.

Things Fall Apart

by Chinua Achebe

£8.99

Things Fall Apart is the debut novel by Nigerian author Chinua Achebe, first published in 1958. Its story chronicles pre-colonial life in the southeastern part of Nigeria and the arrival of Europeans during the late 19th century.

The Fisherman

by Chigozie Obioma

£8.99

The Fishermen is the debut novel by Nigerian author Chigozie Obioma, published in 2015. The novel follows four brothers in a quiet neighbourhood of a Nigerian town, who are given a violent prophecy which shakes their family to the core. It is set in the 1990s, during the rule of Sani Abacha.

Americanah

by Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie

£8.99

A bold, expansive novel that functions perfectly as both love story and articulate political statement about the human cost of military conflict and the effects of globalisation, Americanah pulses with spry, intelligent storytelling and a profound empathy and tenderness. Addressing issues of race and dislocation with grace and wit, Adichie’s masterful novel succeeds on every level.

The beautyful ones are not yet born

by Ayi Kwei Armah

£8.99

The novel is generally a satirical attack on the Ghanaian society during Kwame Nkrumah’s regime and the period immediately after independence in the 1960s.

Part of my soul went with him

by Winnie Mandela

£8.99

Winnie Mandela, one of South Africa's most visible and articulate apartheid foes, spent many years as a "banned" person in her own country. She lived under virtual house arrest and was forbidden to address public gatherings or meet with more than one person at a time. She endured a forced separation of 27 years from her husband, Nelson Mandela. Here, in interviews and letters, she tells the story of her life and political development.

How we fight for our lives

by Saeed Jones

£8.99

A stunning coming-of-age memoir about a young, black, gay man from the South as he fights to carve out a place for himself, within his family, within his country, within his own hopes, desires, and fears. Through a series of vignettes that chart a course across the American landscape, Jones draws readers into his boyhood and adolescence—into tumultuous relationships with his family, into passing flings with lovers, friends, and strangers.
